 EDITORIAL गुरुदेवो महेश्वरः । गुरूव्र ह्मा गुरूविष्णु गुरूसक्षात परब्रह्म, तस्मेः शरी गुरवे नमः Guru is Brahmha, Guru is Vishnu, Guru is Shiva, Verily Guru is Parabrahmha (Primordial father) Salutations to that Guru. India has the finest tradition in the world of Master and Disciple relations. A Guru, like parents, is held in highest esteem. From the very childhood one is told "Acharya Devo Bhava" (May your Teacher be your God). Indian scriptures are replete with the stories of outstanding Masters, like Sages Vasishtha, Atri and meritorious disciples like Aruni, Upamanyu, Ekalavya and others. Guru's words were taken as absolute. Disciples obeyed his orders even at the risk of their lives and Gurus in turn loved them as own children and blessed them with all knowledge. Guru has unique place in man's life. Guidance of a competent master is essential for realisation of self and God. In principle Guru represents holy trinity, Brahmha, Vishnu and Shiva. He is the source of true knowledge, I.e. discrimination between real (Brahmhan) and unreal (illusion). He is 'Dharma' and purity personified and only he can awaken real Dharma through spiritual process and by explaining the scriptures. Adi Shankaracharya describes the genuine Guru as "who is versed in Vedas, sinless, un- smitten by desire, and a knower of Bramhan par excellence, who has withdrawn himself into Bramhan, who is calm like fire that has consumed its fuel (1.e. the state of emer- gence in Bramhan and the perfect cessation of all relative activity), who is boundless re- servoir of mercy that knows no reason and a friend of all good people who prostrate before him." Saints have compared the love of Guru with that of mɔther. Many have described their Gurus as mothers. Sahaja Yogis are the most fortunate seekers to have their mother as their Guru. Cosmic Mother JAGADAMBA, has fulfilled the sweet wishes of saints of having the Guru and Mother in one form. We humbly offer our Puja at Her Lotus Feet. Om Twameva Sakshat Shri Adi Guru Sakshat Shri Bhagwati Mataji Shri Nirmala Devi Namo Namah. Nirmala Yoga 1

New York MOTHER'S NAME- Names are often given under the guidance of the Unconscious and, in this respect H. H. Mataji's names are quite interesting. NIRMALA is the Sanskrit word for IMMACULATE. In her childhood She was called Neera (Born from Water, Miriam, Marie). Her maiden name was SALVE which reminds us of the ritual "Hail Mary" in Latin. Salve regina coeli........ The Spanish speaking guests of H. H. Mataji in Colom bia were surprised at this coinci- dence. After marriage, H.H. Mataji's name became Shrivastava. "Shri" means "the Goddess" (the holy Spirit) and "Vastava" means "in reality" So, by a playful chance, NIRMALA DEVI SHRIVASTAVA reads. THE IMMACULATE GODDESS, THE HOLY SPIRIT IN REALITY.  EDITORIAL
गुरुदेवो महेश्वरः ।
गुरूव्र ह्मा गुरूविष्णु
गुरूसक्षात परब्रह्म, तस्मेः शरी गुरवे नमः
Guru is Brahmha, Guru is Vishnu, Guru is Shiva,
Verily Guru is Parabrahmha (Primordial father)
Salutations to that Guru.
India has the finest tradition in the world of Master and Disciple relations. A Guru,
like parents, is held in highest esteem. From the very childhood one is told "Acharya
Devo Bhava" (May your Teacher be your God). Indian scriptures are replete with the
stories of outstanding Masters, like Sages Vasishtha, Atri and meritorious disciples like
Aruni, Upamanyu, Ekalavya and others. Guru's words were taken as absolute. Disciples
obeyed his orders even at the risk of their lives and Gurus in turn loved them as own
children and blessed them with all knowledge.
Guru has unique place in man's life. Guidance of a competent master is essential
for realisation of self and God. In principle Guru represents holy trinity, Brahmha,
Vishnu and Shiva. He is the source of true knowledge, I.e. discrimination between real
(Brahmhan) and unreal (illusion). He is 'Dharma' and purity personified and only he can
awaken real Dharma through spiritual process and by explaining the scriptures. Adi
Shankaracharya describes the genuine Guru as "who is versed in Vedas, sinless, un-
smitten by desire, and a knower of Bramhan par excellence, who has withdrawn himself
into Bramhan, who is calm like fire that has consumed its fuel (1.e. the state of emer-
gence in Bramhan and the perfect cessation of all relative activity), who is boundless re-
servoir of mercy that knows no reason and a friend of all good people who prostrate
before him."
Saints have compared the love of Guru with that of mɔther. Many have described
their Gurus as mothers. Sahaja Yogis are the most fortunate seekers to have their
mother as their Guru. Cosmic Mother JAGADAMBA, has fulfilled the sweet wishes of
saints of having the Guru and Mother in one form. We humbly offer our Puja at Her
Lotus Feet.
Om Twameva Sakshat Shri Adi Guru Sakshat
Shri Bhagwati Mataji Shri Nirmala Devi Namo Namah.

 -MOTHER-
Mother's Love has opened the gates
for Her to low through,
soothing, cooling,
cleansing, purifying,
filling Her Children with the eternal
nectar.
O Devi I Thou art Ganga
Thou art Jamuna
Who flows everywhere at will.
By Thee all are fulfilled.
O Devi I There is no merit
in renunciation
-indeed it is a myth.
For it is by Thy grace alone
that we cast aside all that once
held us in its grip,
and which is now insipid and dull.
The most brilliant diamond
pales beside the eternal diamond
of Self.
Silver and gold seem worthless
beside the wealth Thou hast bestowed
upon Thy children.
Verily, O Devi, Thou hast freed us
from our shackles.
O Great Deliverer,
O Mahalaxmi,
O Holy Mother.

MOTHER'S NAME-
Names are often given under the guidance of the Unconscious and, in this respect
H. H. Mataji's names are quite interesting.
NIRMALA is the Sanskrit word for IMMACULATE. In her childhood She was called
Neera (Born from Water, Miriam, Marie). Her maiden name was SALVE which reminds
us of the ritual "Hail Mary" in Latin.
Salve regina coeli........
The Spanish speaking guests of H. H. Mataji in Colom bia were surprised at this coinci-
dence. After marriage, H.H. Mataji's name became Shrivastava. "Shri" means "the
Goddess" (the holy Spirit) and "Vastava" means "in reality"
So, by a playful chance, NIRMALA DEVI SHRIVASTAVA reads.
THE IMMACULATE GODDESS, THE HOLY SPIRIT IN REALITY.
Gregoire in-The Advent pp. 169

 PRAY ER
You who Fought again, and won,
the war the angels lost,
You who watched and wept when Christ
was tried and scourged and cross'd,
You who knew the age to come
would fragment and bemuse,
Delude and harass, scare, embarrass,
heartbreak and confuse,
Who chose another human birth
as Mother, Lover, Sage.
To enlighten, brighten, usher
in the Golden age:
Who chose to grant to those who yearned
the gift of love sublime,
Where few become the mass and buds
become the blossom time;
Who chose to show us how to know
the spark that is ourself,
And how to join that grain of God
in each to God Himself;
The union that's been longed for life
by life and heart by heart,
To thank you, Mother, for these gifts
how do we even start ?
But grant us, Mother, even more,
This land of ours enthral,
Make Your Antipodean time
a blessing time for all.
Help us, Mother, if you will,
to grow, to spread, to share,
To reach those souls we know You not
but need Your loving care.
Oh, call again Your Battle cry,
that ringing, stinging AUM,
That on this arid continent
the blossom time may come!
